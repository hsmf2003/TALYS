\section{Conclusion}

This project used TALYS to calculate energy-differential light-ion emission spectra and compared the results with selected EXFOR datasets. The comparison focused on proton production for several targets, and on deuteron and alpha production for silicon. Four pre-equilibrium configurations were tested in order to assess the sensitivity of the predicted spectra to model choices.

The results show that proton production is generally reproduced more smoothly than deuteron production. For proton spectra, the main peak is often reasonably described, while the shoulder and high-energy tail are the most useful regions for distinguishing between pre-equilibrium modes. For deuteron spectra, TALYS shows stronger discrepancies, including sharp structures and abrupt cut-offs at high outgoing energies. The alpha-production case indicates that the absence of low-energy experimental points can limit the ability to constrain the evaporation component.

Overall, the most informative comparisons are those that combine the total spectra, logarithmic-scale plots, ratio plots, and component decompositions. This approach makes it possible not only to identify the best-performing TALYS configuration for each case, but also to understand where and why the model deviates from the experimental data.
