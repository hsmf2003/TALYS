\subsubsection{Degrees and degree distribution}

The results of degree distribution study are given in figure \ref{fig:degree_distribution}. 

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{figs/WS_1_degrees_ver2.png}
    \caption{Degree distribution for a Watts-Strogatz graph with values $N=200$ and $k=6$  for different $p$ values.}
    \label{fig:degree_distribution}
\end{figure}

The graph shows that at $p=0$ every node has $d=k$ as was predicted in the theory. As $p$ gets larger a gaussian shaped distribution starts to emerge. At higher $p$ values the distribution gets wider, showcasing a more randomized graph but the mean value of the distribution still stays around $6.0$ which is the fixed $k$ value.
